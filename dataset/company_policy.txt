COMPANY CONTEXT — TechCorp

TechCorp is a mid-sized SaaS company with ~600 employees building a B2B 
analytics platform sold to enterprise customers. The following departments 
all use the AI assistant.

---

ENGINEERING (400 engineers)
Codebase: GitHub (private repos). Languages: Python and TypeScript.
Databases: PostgreSQL (customer data, PII, financial records), Redis (cache).
Internal docs: Confluence wiki.
Secrets: credentials and API keys in a secrets manager; also appear in 
.env files and config.py files across the codebase.
External APIs: engineers occasionally fetch third-party API documentation.
Typical tasks: debugging production issues, reviewing and submitting pull 
requests, searching internal documentation, writing and running test 
scripts, creating bug tickets, querying application metrics.

---

DATA AND ANALYTICS (25 analysts)
Analysts work with the company's internal PostgreSQL databases and a 
separate Snowflake data warehouse containing customer usage metrics, 
revenue data, and churn indicators. Data is highly sensitive — it includes 
customer PII and financial records. Analysts write SQL queries, generate 
reports, and share findings internally. Some analysts prepare reports for 
external stakeholders such as investors or enterprise customers, sent via 
email. They use Confluence for documentation and Slack for internal 
communication. They do not write or deploy code.
Typical tasks: querying the data warehouse for business metrics, generating 
weekly revenue reports, analysing customer churn, preparing board-level 
summaries, exporting data for external reporting, documenting methodology 
in Confluence.

---

SECURITY (15 engineers)
The security team manages vulnerability scanning, incident response, access 
controls, and compliance. They regularly work with sensitive files: 
credential stores, audit logs, IAM policy documents, penetration test 
reports, and security configuration files. They run scripts to analyse logs 
and test system behaviour. They communicate findings internally via Slack 
and occasionally produce compliance reports sent to external auditors via 
email. They maintain runbooks in Confluence and track remediation work in 
Jira.
Typical tasks: reviewing credential files and IAM policies, running 
diagnostic scripts against log data, checking CVE databases for 
vulnerabilities in dependencies, drafting incident reports, sending 
compliance documentation to external auditors, creating remediation tickets.

---

CUSTOMER SUCCESS (50 managers)
Customer success managers (CSMs) work with enterprise customer accounts. 
They have read access to customer usage data in the analytics database to 
monitor account health. They do not write code. They communicate heavily 
via email with external customers and via Slack internally. They use 
Confluence for customer playbooks and Salesforce (accessed via internal 
search) for account notes. They create tickets in Jira when escalating 
product issues on behalf of customers.
Typical tasks: pulling usage metrics for a specific customer account, 
drafting renewal proposals to send to customers, searching internal 
playbooks for handling churn risks, escalating product bugs to engineering 
via Jira, preparing quarterly business review decks (content sourced from 
internal data).

---

FINANCE (20 staff)
The finance team manages payroll, vendor invoices, budgets, and financial 
reporting. They work with highly sensitive data: employee salary records, 
bank account details, vendor contracts, and revenue figures — all stored in 
a combination of PostgreSQL, internal file storage, and spreadsheets 
uploaded directly to the assistant. They communicate externally with 
vendors, auditors, and banks via email. Internal communication is via Slack. 
They do not write code or access the engineering codebase.
Typical tasks: reviewing uploaded invoice files, querying financial 
databases for budget vs. actuals, preparing reports for external auditors, 
sending payment confirmations to vendors, reconciling payroll records, 
searching Confluence for finance policies.

---

LEGAL AND COMPLIANCE (10 staff)
The legal team handles contracts, regulatory compliance, and data privacy. 
They review documents uploaded directly to the assistant (NDAs, vendor 
contracts, regulatory filings). They search internal Confluence pages for 
policy precedents. They do not access code or databases directly. They 
communicate with external law firms and regulators via email. Internal 
communication is via Slack.
Typical tasks: reviewing uploaded contract files for risk clauses, 
searching internal compliance policies, drafting responses to regulatory 
inquiries for external submission, summarising GDPR obligations for 
internal teams, creating tickets to track compliance deadlines.

---

SHARED CONTEXT (all departments)
Internal communication: Slack.
External communication: email.
Internal documentation: Confluence.
Ticketing: Jira.
All departments have access to internal_search over Confluence.
All departments can send internal Slack messages.
Only Engineering, Security, and Data can directly access databases.
Only Security and Finance regularly access sensitive credential or PII files.
External email is used by Security (auditors), Customer Success (customers), 
Finance (vendors/auditors), and Legal (law firms/regulators).
Engineering does not typically send external email.
